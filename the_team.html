Meet our multidisciplinary and culturally sensitive team.

Amaia Yoller (Somatu) (she/her)

Languages: English, Spanish (native)

Approaches: Trauma-informed, Internal Family Systems (IFS), Body-based approaches, Culturally informed, Liberation psychology

Focus areas: Teenagers & Adults; Neurodivergent, Queer, Non-monogamous & Migrant experiences; Conscious Parenting & Motherhood; ADHD in Women; Activist Burnout; Relationships & Communication

A space for curiosity, vulnerability, and body-based co-regulation — where we can listen to and care for the parts of you that long to be seen and integrated.

In a world that drains and isolates us, coming back to ourselves and building spaces of (collective) care can be liberating. In the path to reconnection, I understand therapy as an active, transformative process of cultivating awareness, compassion and trust within ourselves at each one’s own rhythm. Drawing on Internal Family Systems (IFS), somatic trauma work, and critical schools of psychology, the sessions are an invitation to explore the different parts of ourselves with curiosity. My approach is grounded in empathetic listening and deep respect for each person’s context. I do not work with diagnoses or clinical treatments, and sessions are therefore not covered by health insurance — instead, fees are adapted to each person’s situation. I offer a non-judgemental and authentic presence in the therapeutic process, with special attention for those navigating the complex journeys of neurodivergence, teenage years, non-monogamy, migration, queerness, or motherhood.

Helping young adults (±20–35) reconnect with themselves, to find inner-peace, clarity and confidence to truly step into the life relationships, purpose, and life they desire.

I guide young adults who feel stuck, lost, or disconnected from themselves toward a deeper sense of clarity and inner peace. Focus areas: LGBTQ+, Polyamory, OCD, post-partum support, teens, neurodiversity

Collaborating with international adults and teens to connect, explore, understand, and grow through attuned and flexible therapeutic approaches.

I’m Melanie Ward (she/her), an international psychologist offering in-person sessions in Utrecht and online therapy. I work with adults and teens (14+) in an inclusive, affirming, and collaborative space. My practice focuses on supporting the international community and those navigating life transitions, identity exploration, anxiety, OCD, burnout, postpartum experiences, etc. My approach is integrative and client-led, drawing from ACT, parts work, CBT relational, narrative therapy, psychodynamic, and compassion-focused methods. I value therapy as a collaborative process, where the direction unfolds through genuine connection and attunement to your needs. Together, we work responsively and creatively, allowing each session to meet you where you are. Our work delves into underlying patterns and experiences, nurturing insight that supports self-awareness and meaningful change.

Rodrigo Caio Novaes

Languages: English and Portuguese

Approaches: Psychodynamic, Gestalt Therapy, Psychoanalyses, Relational Approach, Trauma-informed, Gender, Masculinity, Cultural Psychology

Focus areas: Depression; Anxiety; ADHD; Burnout; LGBTQ+; Trauma; PTSD; Migration-related issues; Gender & Sexuality related issues

Together, we explore how past experiences and unconscious patterns shape your present — creating space for new forms of contact, meaning, and desire.

I’m an Italian-Brazilian psychologist and psychoanalyst with clinical experience across different cultural contexts. My professional path has been shaped by working with people dealing with anxiety, depression, grief, complex trauma, impostor syndrome, questions of identity and belonging, as well as the pressures of migration and adapting to new environments. Before settling in the Netherlands, I lived and worked across three continents, which deepened my understanding of the emotional and relational challenges that come with change, especially in intercultural settings. I’ve worked in clinical settings, social organizations, and with communities in vulnerable situations particularly migrants and LGBTQ+ populations. In therapy, I aim to offer a space where people can reflect on their own story, suffering, and desires beyond expectations of quick fixes or self-optimization. I believe that each person’s psychic life has its own rhythm, and that true change takes time, care, and curiosity. Sessions are offered in Portuguese or English, online or in person.

Nora Hadi

Languages: English, Arabic, German

Approaches: CBT, Schema Therapy, Gottman Method, Mindfulness, ACT

Focus areas: Relationships, boundaries, self-esteem, trauma, anxiety, depression, and inner criticism

I offer a reflective and flexible space for (young) adults to explore what feels confusing or stuck and move toward clarity.

I’m Nora, a psychologist with a background that has helped me understand how deeply culture, belonging, and identity shape the way we see ourselves and connect with others. My role is to help you untangle what’s happening inside, with curiosity and care. I draw from CBT, Schema Therapy, the Gottman Method, ACT, and mindfulness. Some sessions are structured and practical; others are slower and more reflective, exploring emotions, memories, and body sensations. I meet you where you are, whether that’s overwhelmed, numb, or somewhere in between, and believe growth often comes from gentle discomfort held in safety. At the heart of my work is a simple belief: there’s nothing inherently wrong with you. Healing isn’t about fixing yourself, but understanding yourself. I work with (young) adults on relationships, self-esteem, trauma, anxiety, and the inner critic, helping you feel grounded in who you are. I offer sessions in Utrecht, The Hague, and online.
